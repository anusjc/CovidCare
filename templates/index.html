Myth-Busters
These are the facts

1: Cold weather and snow CANNOT kill the CoronaVirus .

2: The coronavirus CAN be transmitted in areas with hot and humid climates.

3: The coronavirus CANNOT be transmitted through mosquito bites.

4: There is NO evidence that companion animals/pets such as dogs or cats can transmit the coronavirus.

5: Taking a hot bath DOES NOT prevent the coronavirus.

6: Hand dryers are NOT effective in killing the coronavirus.

7: Ultraviolet light SHOULD NOT be used for sterilization and can cause skin irritation.

8: Thermal scanners CAN detect if people have a fever but CANNOT detect whether or not someone has the coronavirus.

9: Spraying alcohol or chlorine all over your body WILL NOT kill viruses that have already entered your body.

10: Vaccines against pneumonia, such as pneumococcal vaccine and Haemophilus influenzae type b (Hib) vaccine, DO NOT provide protection against the coronavirus.

11: There is NO evidence that regularly rinsing the nose with saline has protected people from infection with the coronavirus.

12: Garlic is healthy but there is NO evidence from the current outbreak that eating garlic has protected people from the coronavirus.

13: Antibiotics DO NOT work against viruses, antibiotics only work against bacteria.

14: To date, there is NO specific medicine recommended to prevent or treat the coronavirus.

Note: Content is sourced from World Health Organization
